## Origin

The Biafran War ended in 1970.

Over a million Igbo people did not survive it. Many of them did not die from bullets. They died from starvation — from aid that was blocked, withheld, and weaponised by those in power. The systems that should have carried food and medicine were controlled by the same forces that caused the harm.

NSIGII is the answer to that. Not in 1970. Now. Here. In code.

---

## The Name

**NSIGII** is pronounced **N-SIGII**.

It is a diacritic stem — derived from **NSIBIDI**, the ancient Igbo writing system in which every symbol carries both a verb and a noun simultaneously. In NSIBIDI, you do not separate the concept from the action. The word *is* the thing it describes.

NSIGII inherits this structure. Every protocol clause is simultaneously a *statement of principle* and a *technical behaviour*. The protocol does not describe ethics. The protocol *is* the ethics, encoded.

## Mission

> So that we all breathe, live, and work.

NSIGII is a humanitarian aid protocol. It governs autonomous systems — drones, networks, robots — that carry food, water, shelter, and medical supplies to people in crisis zones, civil conflict environments, and places where conventional aid systems have failed or been captured.

It is not charity. It is a constitutional right, enforced by architecture.

## The Three Constitutional Clauses

These are not guidelines. They are hard constraints, enforced at every layer of the system.

| Clause | Human State | Technical Behaviour |
|--------|-------------|---------------------|
| **Never a problem** | Human IN the loop | Full operator control. The system does exactly what a human commands and nothing else. |
| **Never a toy** | Human ON the loop | Observer mode. Physics-bound. Consent-verified. The system cannot be trivialised or misused. |
| **Never a weapon** | Human OUT of the loop | No autonomous targeting. No offensive capability. The system cannot be turned against people. |

These three clauses form the **Trilateral SDK** — the constitutional foundation that every NSIGII-compliant system must implement.

## Consensus Protocol — HERE AND NOW

NSIGII operates under a strict temporal priority order:

1. **HERE AND NOW** — first, always. The immediate human need. The person in front of you. The crisis happening now.
2. **THERE AND THEN** — second. Historical context. What happened before. What must not be repeated.
3. **WHEN AND WHERE** — third, by consensus. Future planning. Coordination. Strategy.

This ordering is not arbitrary. It reflects the failure mode that killed people during the Biafran War: systems that prioritised political consensus (WHEN AND WHERE) over immediate human need (HERE AND NOW). NSIGII reverses this. The living person always comes first.

## The Relationship to NSIBIDI

NSIBIDI is one of the oldest known writing systems in West Africa. It is not alphabetic. It is conceptual — each symbol encodes a situation, a relationship, a truth. It was used by the Ejagham people and spread across Igbo and Cross River communities. Colonial powers suppressed it. It survived.

NSIGII inherits that survival. The name is not decoration. The diacritic structure — the way N-SIGII splits the word at the point of stress — mirrors how NSIBIDI encodes meaning at the point of concept. The protocol is written the way the ancestors wrote: with the action and the meaning unified, inseparable.
